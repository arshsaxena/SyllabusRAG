Course Code: BECE302L
Course Title: Control Systems
Prerequisites: BECE202L
Course Objectives:
- To study the use of transfer function model for the analysis of physical systems and to introduce the components of control system.
- To provide adequate knowledge in the time response of systems and steady state error analysis along with the understanding of closed-loop and open-loop system analysis in frequency domain.
- To introduce the design of controllers and compensators for the stability analysis.
- To introduce state variable representation of physical systems and study the stability analysis in state space approach.
Modules:
- Control Systems
- Mathematical Modeling of Physical Systems
- Time Domain Response
- Characterization of Systems
- Frequency Domain Response
- Controllers and Compensators Design
- State Space Analysis
- Contemporary issues

Course Code: BECE306L
Course Title: Digital Communication Systems
Prerequisites: BECE206L, BECE206P
Course Objectives:
- To understand the transmitter and receiver blocks of various waveform coding techniques.
- To analyze various line coding techniques in time and frequency domains.
- To identify the role of baseband, bandpass formats and information theory for effective transmission of signals, combat ISI and to increase the reliability of transmission.
- To understand the principles and importance of spread spectrum and multiple access in the context of communication.
Modules:
- Sampling Process
- Waveform Coding Techniques
- Line Codes
- Baseband System
- Bandpass system
- Spread Spectrum and Multiple Access Techniques
- Introduction to Information Theory
- Contemporary issues
